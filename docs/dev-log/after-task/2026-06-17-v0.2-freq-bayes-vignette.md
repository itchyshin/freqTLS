# After-task: frequentist-vs-Bayesian article (v0.2 vignette)

## Task goal

A standalone conceptual vignette (maintainer-requested) on the strengths and
weaknesses of the likelihood (profileTLS) and Bayesian (bayesTLS) paths for the
same TLS model: priors, identifiability, non-convergence, interval meaning, and
-- a point the maintainer specifically asked for -- how clamping, shrinkage, and
penalties behave like implicit priors, with proper references.

## What was written

`vignettes/frequentist-and-bayesian.Rmd` (html_vignette, builds without Stan):

1. One model, two inferential philosophies.
2. Priors: regularisation vs sensitivity (and the cost of being prior-free).
3. Identifiability: profileTLS *reveals* weak data (open profile, warnings,
   hollow Confidence Eye) vs a prior can *mask* it (a closed credible interval
   that is largely the prior). Live illustration: a sparse 2-temperature fit
   whose `z` profile is `open_both` (NA both sides).
4. Non-convergence and diagnostics: optimiser code / non-pd Hessian / non-closing
   profile + BFGS and bootstrap fallbacks, vs MCMC divergences / ESS / R-hat +
   reparam/priors (table).
5. Compatibility vs credible intervals (the language discipline).
6. **The blurry line: clamping, shrinkage, and penalties as implicit priors** --
   profileTLS's own p-clamp / beta boundary clamp / Beta shape floors (~a
   degenerate boundary prior; the separation cure), the RE random intercept =
   Gaussian prior / partial pooling / empirical-Bayes BLUP shrinkage, and
   penalised likelihood = MAP (ridge=Gaussian, lasso=Laplace, Firth=Jeffreys).
   Live illustration: no-pooling vs partial-pooling colony-effect spread (the
   BLUPs pulled in by the RE Gaussian prior).
7. Agreement on strong data / divergence on weak data.
8. Practical guidance: when to reach for which (complementary, not a winner).

## References

Fourteen citations + Bishop (2006) for the penalty=MAP equivalence, all verified
(authors, year, title, venue, DOI) by a literature-curation pass against
Crossref/publisher pages: James & Stein 1961; Efron & Morris 1975; Robinson 1991
(BLUP); Hoerl & Kennard 1970 (ridge); Tibshirani 1996 (lasso); Park & Casella
2008 (Bayesian lasso); Firth 1993 (Jeffreys penalty / separation); Gelman et al.
2008 (weakly informative priors / separation); Chung et al. 2013 (penalised
variance components); Cole, Chu & Greenland 2014 (ML/profile/penalised primer);
Hastie, Tibshirani & Friedman 2009; Gelman & Hill 2007; Bishop 2006 (MAP =
penalised likelihood); Rafi & Greenland 2020 and Amrhein et al. 2019
(compatibility language; the latter noted as a Nature Comment). DOIs included.
