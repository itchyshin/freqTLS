## What was added

A capstone section in `vignettes/comparing-to-bayesTLS.Rmd`, *Beyond the matched
shape: stage-specific curves (v0.2)*, after the three-way benchmark and before the
"when to prefer which" guidance. It re-fits `zebrafish_lethal` with stage-specific
shapes (grouped `low` / `up` / `log_k`) and compares to the shared-shape
(benchmark) configuration by AIC, then shows the shrimp derivations.

## Finding (real, verified output)

- Shared-shape AIC **1221.8** (df 10) vs stage-specific-shape **1187.0** (df 16):
  ΔAIC **34.8** favouring stage-specific shapes.
- The gain is concentrated in the upper asymptote `up` (maximum survival): young
  embryos **0.718** (SE 0.032) vs old embryos **0.918** (SE 0.015), larvae
  **0.939** (SE 0.025). Young embryos have a markedly lower survival ceiling -- a
  biological signal the matched constant-shape configuration (the two-stage
  workflow and the `bayesTLS` benchmark here) structurally cannot express. `k`
  (steepness) is similar across stages (7.8-8.8); CTmax ordering old 41.3 > young
  40.0 > larvae 39.8 matches known biology.
- Shrimp derivations off the same fitted curve: `derive_ctmax(0.5, 1 h)` = 31.73
  C; survival under 32 C for 4 h = 0.017 (near-total mortality, as 32 C is above
  the fitted CTmax 31.77). `derive_tcrit()` mentioned in prose (lethal-endpoint
  caveat).
