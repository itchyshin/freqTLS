# After-task: general continuous covariates on the shape parameters (item 4)

## Task goal

Support a general (continuous) covariate on a shape sub-parameter, e.g.
`log_k ~ body_size`, independently of the other shapes and of `CTmax`/`log_z` --
end to end (fit, estimates, predict, confint). Re-attempted carefully after a
process crash corrupted the first attempt; the byte-identical C++ foundation was
committed separately first (`4987423`).

## Why it was larger than the handoff implied

The handoff framed item 4 as "rework `tls_predict_pars()`, R-only, medium". In
fact the R side threaded a single `n_shape` and the **CTmax** group levels for
ALL shapes, assuming shapes share the CTmax grouping; and the engine's
`shared_shape` flag keyed off `beta_low` only, so a `log_k` covariate was ignored
when `low` was intercept-only. So it needed a byte-identical-gated C++ change plus
a per-shape deconflation across the name map, estimates, prediction, and confint.

## What changed

- **`src/profile_tls.cpp`** (committed as `4987423`, byte-identical): per-shape
  `low_shared` / `gap_shared` / `logk_shared` detection and per-observation paths;
  the report block sizes `low`/`up`/`k` to their own coefficient vectors, with a
  per-column `up` only when `low` and `gap` share a width. Verified bit-identical
  (max|diff| = 0) for binomial / grouped / beta-binomial.
- **`R/fit_tls.R`**: per-shape widths `n_low`/`n_gap`/`n_logk`; `tls_default_start`
  takes the three widths; `tls_estimates` rewritten to classify each shape by its
  own design -- scalar (natural), one-hot grouping (natural per group), or general
  (LINK-scale `beta_*` coefficients with SE from the sdreport FIXED block). Stores
  `shape_terms` on the fit.
- **`R/utils.R`**: `tls_is_onehot()` helper; `tls_name_map()` per-shape (names
  general coefficients `k:<col>` etc., link scale).
- **`R/formula.R`**: the same-design / match-CTmax constraint REMOVED; captures the
  per-shape right-hand sides as `shape_terms` so predict can rebuild designs.
- **`R/predict.R`**: `tls_predict_pars()` rebuilds a shape design from `newdata`
  (`tls_design_from_rhs(shape_terms[[role]], newdata, ...)`) and applies the
  forward map when the shape is not a scalar and not the CTmax one-hot grouping;
  otherwise the byte-identical group lookup.
- **`R/confint.R`**: general (group = NA) shape coefficients route to Wald (like
  `up` / `sigma_CTmax`) -- the map-refit profile target resolver has no group
  level to address -- and the Wald interval is computed on the LINK scale from the
  estimates table (NOT back-transformed: a slope is not a natural-scale `k`).

## Design decisions

- General-design shape coefficients are reported and interval-ed on the **link
  scale** (`k:<col>` is a log-scale slope, `low:<col>` a logit-scale coefficient),
  matching standard GLM coefficient reporting. One-hot grouped shapes keep their
  natural per-group values (byte-identical).
- Profile intervals for continuous slopes are routed to **Wald** (documented),
  consistent with `up` and `sigma_CTmax`. Profiling a single continuous slope is a
  possible future refinement.

## Checks run and exact outcomes

- Byte-identical: binomial / grouped-shape / beta-binomial fits reproduce the
  committed estimates to **max|diff| = 0** (vs a HEAD baseline) after recompiling
  the engine and the R rework.
- `devtools::test()` -> `[ FAIL 0 | WARN 0 | SKIP 0 | PASS 423 ]` (was 410; +8 new
  acceptance tests in `test-shape-continuous.R`, +2 from updating the two
  constraint-rejection tests to assert the relaxed behaviour, +3 strengthening).
- Acceptance (`log_k ~ x`, truth slope 0.6): fit conv 0; `k:x` = 0.575 (link/log
  scale); predict applies the design (survival varies with `x`); Wald CI
  [0.463, 0.688] brackets the estimate; `confint(method="profile")` routes to Wald.
- `rcmdcheck(... _R_CHECK_FORCE_SUGGESTS_=false ...)` -> recorded in the check-log.

## Known limitations / next

- Profile (not Wald) for a continuous slope is deferred.
- A shape grouped by a factor OTHER than the CTmax grouping fits, and predict
  rebuilds it from `newdata`; thorough coverage of that combo is light.
