# Figure Audit: Phase 4 Confidence Eye + survival plots (rendered-PNG inspection)

## Date

2026-06-16 (Florence)

## Scope

Render-proof for the Phase 4 plots: every figure below was generated with
`ggplot2::ggsave()` and then the *actual PNG file was read back and visually
inspected*. No figure is signed off on code alone. The Confidence-Eye contract
(SPEC.md S13) is the acceptance standard: pale low-alpha compatibility lens,
hollow white point estimate (shape 21), compatibility/confidence language only
(never "posterior"/"credible"), caption exposing the interval source + scale,
and an honest fallback that refuses to draw a lens for a non-closing profile.

## Fixtures

- Headline fit: `simulate_tls(family="binomial", CTmax=36, z=4, seed=1)` then
  `fit_tls(..., family="binomial", tref=1)`. MLE CTmax 35.93, z 4.00.
- Sparse fit (for the fallback): `simulate_tls(temps=c(35,36), times=c(1,2),
  reps=2, n=10, CTmax=36, z=4, seed=9)`.

## What was inspected

### `/tmp/ptls_survival_p4.png` -- `plot_survival_curves(f)` -- PASS

Seven fitted curves (viridis "C", dark-blue 30C -> yellow 42C) on a log10
duration x-axis, y "Survival probability" clamped to [0,1]. Each curve declines
monotonically. The 30C curve sits near 1.0 across all durations (cold = high
survival); the 42C curve sits near 0.0 (hot = near-total mortality); the
intermediate temperatures show the 4PL sigmoid drop, with 34-36C falling across
the observed duration window. Observed survival proportions are overlaid as
solid points that track their temperature's curve. Title "Fitted survival vs
duration"; caption notes the 4PL fit, observed points, and `tref = 1`. Correct
thermal-load behaviour; nothing misleading.

### `/tmp/ptls_eye_p4.png` -- `plot_confidence_eye(f, parm=c("CTmax","z"))` -- PASS

Two facets (CTmax, z), free scales. Each shows a **pale-green lens** (the
compatibility region; `geom_polygon`, alpha 0.35, colour NA) with a **hollow
point** (white interior, dark-green stroke, shape 21) at the estimate
(CTmax ~ 35.93 on a 35.7-36.1 axis; z ~ 4.0 on a 3.6-4.4 axis). The lenses are
widest at the centre and taper toward the interval bounds (the Gaussian-width
lens). Legend lists the three reliability states ("interval open",
"Wald/delta interval", "compatibility interval") with a hollow-circle key.
Caption: "95% compatibility intervals (profile likelihood). Interval scale:
identity, log. Eye = pale lens; hollow point = estimate." No filled points, no
horizontal CI bars, no centre line through the eye, no posterior-density
styling, no "posterior"/"credible" wording. Contract satisfied.

### `/tmp/ptls_eye_open_p4.png` -- honest fallback on a non-closing profile -- PASS

Built from the sparse fit, where `confint(fs, c("CTmax","z"), "profile")` returns
CTmax `open_lower` (conf.low NA) and z `open_both` (both NA). The rendered figure
shows **only a hollow red point per facet and NO lens** (confirmed both visually
and by `any(vapply(g$layers, \(L) inherits(L$geom,"GeomPolygon"), logical(1)))
== FALSE`). Subtitle: "No interval closed; hollow points only (see
`?confint.profile_tls`)." This is the gllvmTMB honest fallback adapted to
`conf.status`: a weakly identified parameter is never drawn as a fabricated
closed eye.

### `/tmp/ptls_eye_line_p4.png` -- `plot_confidence_eye(f, style="line")` -- PASS

Same two facets, but the lens is replaced by a thin green vertical
compatibility-interval line with the hollow point on top. The `style = c("eye",
"line")` switch works; default is "eye".

### `/tmp/ptls_surface_p4.png` -- `plot_survival_surface(f)` -- PASS

Filled viridis heatmap over temperature x log10(duration): survival high
(yellow) at low temp / short duration, low (dark) at high temp / long duration,
with white contour lines tracing the iso-survival diagonal. Fill limits [0,1].
Correct gradient.

### `/tmp/ptls_tdt_p4.png` -- `plot_tdt_curve(f)` -- PASS

A straight declining line on a log10 y-axis: duration-to-50%-survival from ~30 h
at 30C to ~0.03 h at 42C. The log-linearity is expected and correct -- at the
relative midpoint `log10(LT50) = mid = log10(tref) - (temp - CTmax)/z`, linear in
temperature with slope `-1/z` -- which confirms `derive_lt()` reads off the
forward map.

## Verdict

All six rendered figures pass the figure-quality gate by direct image
inspection. The Confidence Eye is the default uncertainty display, uses
compatibility language exclusively, and falls back honestly to hollow points
when a profile does not close. Florence signs off Phase 4 figures.
